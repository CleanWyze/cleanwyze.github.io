Cleaning Checklist

General Cleaning

  • Dusting: Dust all surfaces, including furniture, shelves, picture frames, and light fixtures.
  • Vacuuming/Sweeping: Vacuum or sweep floors and carpets, paying attention to corners and under furniture.
  • Mopping: Mop hard floors, ensuring they are clean and free of dirt or stains.
  • Window Cleaning: Clean windows and window sills, removing smudges and dust.
  • Mirror Cleaning: Clean mirrors and glass surfaces to a shine.
  • Trash Removal: Empty trash and recycling bins.

Specific Room Cleaning

Kitchen

  • Clean countertops, sink, and stovetop.
  • Clean the exterior of appliances (refrigerator, oven, microwave, dishwasher).
  • Clean the inside of the microwave.
  • Wipe down cabinets and drawers.
  • Clean and sanitize the sink and faucet.
  • Spot clean walls and the exterior of kitchen cabinets.

Bathrooms

  • Clean and sanitize toilets, showers, and bathtubs.
  • Clean sinks and countertops.
  • Clean mirrors and glass surfaces.
  • Clean and disinfect light switches and doorknobs.

Bedrooms

  • Make beds and ensure linens are clean.
  • Vacuum or sweep floors and carpets.
  • Dust furniture and shelves.
  • Clean windows and window sills.

Living Areas

  • Dust furniture and shelves.
  • Vacuum upholstered furniture.
  • Clean windows and window sills.
  • Clean picture frames.
  • Wipe down light fixtures, switches, and outlets.

Laundry Room

  • Clean and sanitize the washing machine and dryer.
  • Wipe down shelves and walls in laundry closets.

Additional Tasks

  • Decluttering: Organize and tidy up areas, removing clutter and organizing items.
  • Disinfecting: Sanitize high-touch surfaces, such as doorknobs, light switches, and countertops.
  • Organizing: Organize shelves, drawers, and closets.
  • Deep Cleaning: Depending on the cleaning service, a deep clean might include tasks like cleaning hard-to-reach areas, washing HVAC vent covers, and removing stains.

Why Choose Soft Washing?

Soft washing your home offers a gentler, safer, and more effective cleaning method than traditional pressure washing, protecting delicate surfaces, eliminating mold and mildew, and extending the lifespan of your home's exterior.

Here's a more detailed look at the benefits:

  • Safer for Delicate Surfaces: Soft washing uses low-pressure water and biodegradable cleaning solutions, making it ideal for fragile surfaces like wood siding, stucco, and shingles.
  • Effective Mold and Mildew Removal: Soft washing solutions contain biocides that kill and remove mold, mildew, algae, and organic matterâ€”protecting your home's health and appearance.
  • Long-Lasting Clean: Soft washing provides a deeper clean that lasts longer than pressure washing by addressing the root cause of staining.
  • Eco-Friendly: Most soft washing solutions are biodegradable and less harmful to the environment compared to typical pressure washing chemicals.
  • Improves Curb Appeal: A clean, well-maintained exterior significantly boosts your home's visual appeal and value.
  • Extends Lifespan of Home Surfaces: Regular soft washing helps preserve your siding, roof, and other surfaces by removing damaging contaminants.
  • Reduces Risk of Damage: Its gentle method minimizes the risk of costly surface damage, especially on older or delicate homes.
  • Cleans Hard-to-Reach Areas: Perfect for tricky spots like roofs, eaves, and corners that high-pressure washing often misses.
