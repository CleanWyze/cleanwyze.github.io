Why Choose Soft Washing?

Soft washing your home offers a gentler, safer, and more effective cleaning method than traditional pressure washing, protecting delicate surfaces, eliminating mold and mildew, and extending the lifespan of your home's exterior.

Here's a more detailed look at the benefits:

  • Safer for Delicate Surfaces: Soft washing uses low-pressure water and biodegradable cleaning solutions, making it ideal for fragile surfaces like wood siding, stucco, and shingles.
  • Effective Mold and Mildew Removal: Soft washing solutions contain biocides that kill and remove mold, mildew, algae, and organic matter—protecting your home's health and appearance.
  • Long-Lasting Clean: Soft washing provides a deeper clean that lasts longer than pressure washing by addressing the root cause of staining.
  • Eco-Friendly: Most soft washing solutions are biodegradable and less harmful to the environment compared to typical pressure washing chemicals.
  • Improves Curb Appeal: A clean, well-maintained exterior significantly boosts your home's visual appeal and value.
  • Extends Lifespan of Home Surfaces: Regular soft washing helps preserve your siding, roof, and other surfaces by removing damaging contaminants.
  • Reduces Risk of Damage: Its gentle method minimizes the risk of costly surface damage, especially on older or delicate homes.
  • Cleans Hard-to-Reach Areas: Perfect for tricky spots like roofs, eaves, and corners that high-pressure washing often misses.

Roof Algae 101

The unsightly dark areas of your roof are likely a type of blue green algae known as Gloeocapsa Magma. It releases spores that easily travel from roof to roof, which can wreak havoc on an entire neighborhood.

Roof algae thrives in high humidity, damp areas which are usually shaded. You may notice that the north side of your home or building contains the most green areas. That is because the north side of your structure gets the least amount of sunlight.

Algae and Limestone: A Moisture Trap

Algae cannot live on limestone alone — it also needs water. Algae has an amazing ability to trap and retain moisture in the right conditions.

This damage to your roof can impact your cooling costs, as the moisture in the algae traps heat along with moisture.

Brick Washing

Brick is a very durable material that can safely withstand high temperatures and pressure when compared to other exterior materials.

That doesn't mean that we go with the nuclear option from the get-go. Instead of just power-blasting your surfaces clean, we let it soak in solution in order to penetrate and pre-dissolve the nasty stuff.

This ensures that we don't have to apply surface-damaging levels of pressure to get the job done.

Stucco and Vinyl Washing

Stucco and vinyl siding are much more prone to damage, so we use a process called softwashing. Hot temperatures can damage stucco and vinyl, so the water that we use is cold.

Cleaning more delicate materials such as these is best done with soaking and chemicals, not pressure. We use a perfect blend of chemical concentration, soak time, and light pressure in order to effectively clean these surfaces without doing any damage.

The detergents that we use contain surfactants and rinse aides, similar to a dishwashing liquid. It works great on windows and siding. We also utilize algicide which helps to prevent the growth of more algae in the future.
